Our solar system consists of planets, asteroids, comets, and other objects revolving around the Sun. Some of these objects have moons and other satellites that revolving around them. Much of what we know about the solar system (and beyond) comes from information gathered from telescopes, probes, and other instruments designed and built by Earthlings. Use this tool to learn a little about the planets and other revolving objects they explore.

Earth

Distance from Sun: 92,752,000 miles
Distance in A.U.: 1
Diameter: 7874 miles

Earth is a strange planet where the chemical substance known as water can exist in three phases: solid, liquid, and gaseous. This turns out to be an ideal situation for sustaining weird gooey things known as organisms that come to life with a little spark. One of these organisms, known as humans, build instruments and spacecrafts that they use to explore space. Much of the information here comes from the work of these creatures.
